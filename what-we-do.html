Our Work

What We Do

COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT

Group support, we at YAFA fully support members of the community to form social groups/clubs and have allowed these groups to use the nu base booking to establish these gatherings. For example, we currently have a women's club that meets every Saturday evening, a men's mental health group that meets every Thursday evening, a girls club and a youth club, a number of breakfast groups, we support a very successful basic english classes for BAME women and we also established GCSE Support for young people. We' re always happy to support community initiatives as this is one of our core reasons for setting up the charity. So if you have an idea and need advice and guidance and a facility to deliver the project, please get in contact.

EDUCATION AND TRAINING

English classes for women is one of our most successful projects. We have one qualified English teacher, teaching two classes a week, one beginner/basic level and the other intermediate level; however, class sizes are limited to approximately 14 students. All the women are from the BAME community of Netherthorpe and Upperthorpe.

The B.O.A.T offers short course English classes for all individuals'. Courses are designed for all ages and abilities. We offer alternative ways of learning. For more information click HERE.

YAFA also run in partnership with Sheffield City Council ESOL (English to Speakers of Other Languages) classes, please click HERE to see when classes are available.

GCSE support was set up to help meet that objective. The sessions take place two days a week, Tuesday and Thursday evening and deliver maths and science GCSE support. Staff are all volunteers and are all qualified and experienced teachers.

YOUTH ACTIVITIES

The youth club is held every Friday evening. Currently, we have 15 attendees between the ages of 16-25.

YAFA currently run two Girls club held every Monday (13-18) and Sunday (18+) evenings, both clubs have over 20 attendees and are run by volunteers.

ARTS, CULTURE AND HERITAGE

The local community requested we set up an Arabic school. It's our third year, and we now have seven teachers and 110 students. The school opens Thursday evening between 5.30 PM - 7.30 PM and Saturday between 11AM - 2PM. If you wish your child to attend please register your interest HERE

SPORTS
